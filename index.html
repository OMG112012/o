The Story

Beneath the Surface

On a university campus that looks the way good lies look — polished, sunny, frictionless — six students orbit each other and a fountain in the middle of the quad. From the outside they are fine. From the inside, each one is carrying something they have never said out loud.

Beneath the Surface follows them across one slow-burning semester in which the performances stop holding. Not all at once. Not dramatically. Just a hairline crack in each of them, in a different week, until the weight finally arrives at the surface.

Mental Health University Life Coming-of-Age Identity Grief Recovery Friendship
"This is a book about the loneliness of being surrounded. About the particular ache of being seen without being known."

The final chapter doesn't promise that anyone is healed. It promises something quieter and more honest: that the performance is finally over.

Read it if you have ever sat in a group of people and felt completely alone.
