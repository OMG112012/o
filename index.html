// 05 — The Six

The Characters

Six planets. One dead star. An orbit held together by everything they refuse to say.

01 ·
Luke
The Achiever

He's been awake since four-thirty every morning for six weeks. His planner looks like an evidence board in a murder investigation — colour-coded, dense, and held together by the terror of never being enough. He tells everyone he got an eighty-eight on his midterm. He got a seventy-three.

02 ·
Stephanie
Luke's Sister

She moves like someone crossing a room full of tripwires only she can see. Luke's sister — though nobody at school knows it. She understands exactly what is happening to her body. Understanding it does nothing to stop it.

03 ·
Marcus
The Anchor

He used to fill rooms without trying. Then his father became a phone number he stopped calling. Now he carries what he calls The Static — a low-frequency hum of meaninglessness that has been getting heavier for three years. He went to see Leo's mother. He hasn't told anyone what she said.

04 ·
Emily
The Invisible One

She sits with her sleeves pulled to her knuckles in August heat. She learned early that the way to keep peace was to cost nothing. She's been seeing a counsellor — Dr. Reyes — for four months and told no one. She was waiting until she had something to report. She is still waiting for that moment.

05 ·
Liv
The Performer

She didn't arrive. She detonated. Her laugh fills all available space the way sound does when something is terrified of silence. At the bottom of her bag is an acceptance letter from Whitmore University, twelve hundred miles away. She's been carrying it for three weeks. She hasn't told a single person.

06 ·
Tristan
The Vigilant

His eyes assess the fountain as a non-threat, then move to the perimeter. He is performing wellness with the commitment of a method actor who's been in character so long he can't find the door out. He found a note in Leo's dorm the morning after. He read it. He put it back. He told no one.

// 07 — The Minds

The Creators

Three souls. One shared obsession with the things people carry quietly. We wrote this book because we couldn't find the words anywhere else — so we made them.

Mariam
Co-Author

She has always been the one who notices things — the pause before someone answers, the way a room changes when certain people enter it. She wrote this to make space for the feelings that don't have clean names yet.

Omar
Co-Author

He grew up watching people pretend. He's been trying to write about that for years. This book is the first time it came out right — complicated, uncomfortable, more honest than he expected.

Ganah
Co-Author

She used to journal obsessively — pages and pages of things she would never say out loud. A lot of that is in here. She is terrified and relieved that it exists now, somewhere outside of her.
