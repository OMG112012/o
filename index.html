// 05 — The Six

The Characters

Six planets. One dead star. An orbit held together by everything they refuse to say.

01 ·
Luke
The Achiever

He has been awake since four-thirty every morning for six weeks. His planner looks like an evidence board in a murder investigation — colour-coded, dense, held together by the terror of never being enough. Nobody knows any of it yet.

02 ·
Stephanie
Luke's Sister

She moves like someone crossing a room full of tripwires only she can see. She understands exactly what is happening to her body and her mind. Understanding it does nothing to stop it.

03 ·
Marcus
The Anchor

He used to fill rooms without trying. Then his father became a phone number he stopped calling. Now he carries what he calls The Static — a low hum of weight that has been getting heavier for three years running.

04 ·
Emily
The Invisible One

She sits with her sleeves pulled to her knuckles in August heat. She started seeing a counsellor four months ago and hasn't told anyone. She was waiting until she had something to report. She is still waiting.

05 ·
Liv
The Performer

She didn't arrive. She detonated. Her laugh fills all available space the way sound does when something is terrified of silence. At the bottom of her bag is a letter from a university twelve hundred miles away that she hasn't told a single person about.

06 ·
Tristan
The Vigilant

His eyes hit the fountain, assess it as a non-threat, then move to the perimeter. He is performing wellness with the commitment of a method actor who has been in character so long he can't find the door out.

// 07 — The Minds

The Creators

Three souls. One shared obsession with the things people carry quietly. We wrote this book because we couldn't find the words anywhere else — so we made them.

Mariam
Co-Author

She has always been the one who notices things — the pause before someone answers, the way a room changes when certain people enter it. She wrote this to make space for the feelings that don't have clean names yet.

Omar
Co-Author

He grew up watching people pretend. He has been trying to write about that for years. This book is the first time it came out right — complicated, uncomfortable, more honest than he expected.

Ganah
Co-Author

She used to journal obsessively — pages and pages of things she would never say out loud. A lot of that is in here. She is terrified and relieved that it exists now, somewhere outside of her.
